RyuNova Build Proposal Content

Plan for client-facing proposal – Coffee Machine Warehouse

Assurance, agile delivery, 2-month MVP sequence

Purpose

Create proposal content to present to the coffee machine client (e.g. GLOBAL COFFEE EMPORIUM PTY LTD / Coffee Machine Warehouse) so they can approve building the RyuNova Platform application. The proposal must give assurance that the application will make their staff's life easier, set out key messages, and describe an agile, 2-month build with a clear MVP sequence.

Context: Client sells new and used coffee machines, has limited staff, struggles to keep eBay and website (e.g. Shopify) updated. This proposal is for building the RyuNova Platform application (listing, channel integration, order review, fulfillment).

1. Assurance: how the application makes staff life easier

The proposal will state these assurance points clearly (in executive summary and benefits section):

AssuranceExplanation for client
One place for productsEnter or edit a product once; the system can push it to website (Shopify), eBay, and other channels. No more copying titles, prices, and descriptions between platforms.
Controlled, visible listingStaff choose which products go to which channel with simple "list here" flags. Listing status (draft, listed, ended, sold) is visible in one dashboard.
Orders in one placeOrders from eBay, Shopify, and other channels brought into one list for review and fulfillment, with source channel kept for traceability.
Less manual work, fewer errorsFewer manual updates and copy-paste mistakes; consistent product and pricing data across channels.
Time back for the teamFocus on sales and customer service instead of repetitive listing and sync tasks.
Built in small stepsDelivered in small, working increments (MVPs) over 2 months so the client sees progress early and can give feedback.

2. Key points of communication

  • What we're proposing – Build RyuNova Platform: product catalogue, push listings to channels (Shopify, eBay, etc.), orders in one place for review and fulfillment.
  • Why it helps your team – One product entry, multiple channels; clear listing status; one order list; less manual work; time back for staff.
  • How we'll work – Agile: short iterations (e.g. 2-week sprints), working software at each MVP, demos and feedback, scope prioritised with you.
  • What you'll see and when – 2-month timeline with 3–4 MVPs; after each MVP something usable (product management, then listing, then orders).
  • What we need from you – Access to channels (Shopify, eBay), product/category decisions, availability for demos and prioritisation (e.g. every 2 weeks).
  • In and out of scope – In: product master, listing to channels, order import and fulfillment view, basic inventory. Out (or later): 3PL, advanced reporting, shipping labels.
  • Next step – Confirm scope and MVP order; schedule kick-off and first sprint.

3. Agile method

  • Iterations (sprints): Time-boxed (e.g. 2 weeks); each produces a potentially shippable increment.
  • Prioritisation: Backlog by value and dependency; client joins short prioritisation/review.
  • Demos and feedback: At end of each MVP (or every 2 weeks); feedback feeds next iteration.
  • Change and scope: New ideas welcomed; re-prioritise backlog; protect "must have in 2 months" items.
  • Transparency: Progress visible via working software and "done" vs "next".

Application built over 2 months as a sequence of Minimum Viable Products (MVPs). Month 1 = MVP1 + MVP2; Month 2 = MVP3 + MVP4.

Dependencies: MVP2 depends on MVP1; MVP3 once at least one channel is listing; MVP4 builds on MVP3.

5. Steps to build progressively

  1. Kick-off and alignment – Confirm scope, MVP order, channel priorities. Agree demo and feedback cadence (e.g. every 2 weeks).
  2. MVP1 (Weeks 1–2) – Foundation: product master, images, categories, auth, Product CRUD UI. Demo: "You can manage your products in one place."
  3. MVP2 (Weeks 3–4) – Listing: channel config, list/delist, status. Demo: "You can list selected products to [channel(s)] and see status here."
  4. MVP3 (Weeks 5–6) – Orders: import and single list, fulfillment status. Demo: "You can see and manage orders from all channels in one place."
  5. MVP4 (Weeks 7–8) – Inventory visibility and polish; fixes and handover. Demo: "You have inventory visibility and a stable app ready for daily use."
  6. Handover – Documentation, access, optional support period.

6. Deliverable

Output: A single proposal document (e.g. RyuNova/PROPOSAL_BUILD_RYUNOVA_CHANNELS.md) containing: title and client name; executive summary (assurance + 2-month agile MVPs); how the application makes staff life easier; key communication points; agile method; two-month timeline and MVP sequence; steps to build progressively; scope in/out, assumptions, what we need from you, next steps. Optional: cost or effort if client expects it.

Placement: Under RyuNova/ so all "build this application" content is in one place; proposal can still say "Prepared for Coffee Machine Warehouse".
